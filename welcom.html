History of Hobby Horse, a Timeless Pastime

Hobby horse or hobbyhorsing is a recreational activity that involves using a stick with a plush or fabric horse head attached to it. The origins of this traditional pastime can be traced back to antiquity. It was first documented in the 12th century in France, where it was known as "la chevalerie" and used as a children's game. Other versions of the game have been discovered in Europe and Asia. The modern version of the hobby horse likely emerged in the 18th century in England and Germany, when children began crafting their own wooden horses from sticks with carved animal heads attached. In England, these leisure horses were called "toy-horses" or "stick-horses." This traditional game gradually spread throughout Europe, becoming popular in both rural and urban areas. During this period, recreational riding became associated with equestrian events and competitions among the nobility and royalty. In recent decades, hobby horsing has experienced a resurgence of interest worldwide, primarily due to its social appeal and potential for personal expression. It has become popular among not only children who enjoy riding but also young adults, even those past the age of 18, who love to not only ride but also create their own unique equestrian stories through choreography and costume design. Hobby horse enthusiasts often form tight-knit communities that share ideas, training tips, and support each other through creative collaborations.

Hobby horsing competitions !

But hobby horsing is not just an individual discipline. It's also an opportunity to come together with other enthusiasts and organize hobby horsing competitions. These events can take various forms, ranging from informal competitions among friends to more official contests with judges and performance categories. These competitions are highly developed in some countries around the world, such as Finland, where hobby horsing has become a genuine sport. Participants in these competitions can showcase their show jumping or dressage routines in front of an ever-enthusiastic audience. It's a chance to demonstrate your skills, compete with others, and receive constructive feedback for improvement, all while maintaining a lighthearted atmosphere.
